name: gap-check
description: Check whether reading notes cover all the key questions from the research brief before outlining
user-invocable: true
---

Check reading note coverage against the research brief's key questions.

1. Read `stages/01_research/output/research-brief.md` — specifically the "Key questions" and "Sub-topics to explore" sections.
2. Read `stages/01_research/output/reading-notes.md` — the author's accumulated notes from their reading.
3. For each key question in the research brief, assess whether the reading notes contain enough material to answer it: **Covered** / **Partial** / **Missing**.
4. Produce a coverage table:

   | Question | Status | Notes |
   |---|---|---|
   | [question from brief] | Covered / Partial / Missing | What's there, or what's lacking |

5. For each Partial or Missing question, suggest the source type most likely to fill the gap (referencing the "Suggested source types" section of the research brief).
6. Close with one of:
   - "All key questions are covered. You're ready to run Stage 2."
   - "[N] questions need more material. Read further, then re-run /gap-check, or proceed to Stage 2 and note the gaps in the outline."
